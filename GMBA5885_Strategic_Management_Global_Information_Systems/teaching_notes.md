# Teaching Notes – GMBA 5885 Strategic Management Global Information Systems

## Course Overview

GMBA 5885 is an MBA-level course on the strategic and managerial role of information systems in organizations.

As my first MBA-level teaching assignment, this course has undergone substantial iterative refinement across repeated offerings. Teaching this course significantly shaped my understanding of MBA pedagogy, executive/professional education, and effective instructional design for experienced managerial audiences.

---

## Pedagogical Philosophy and Course Positioning

Through repeated offerings, I developed a clearer understanding of the appropriate positioning of this course and of MBA instruction more broadly.

### Core Positioning of the Course

I view effective MBA instruction in this course as balancing two core objectives:

#### 1. Industry Relevance
Course content should connect directly to real managerial and organizational problems faced by working professionals.

#### 2. Intellectual Challenge
MBA education should remain academically rigorous and intellectually demanding, extending beyond purely practical training.

---

### Role of the Instructor in MBA Education

A key pedagogical realization from teaching this course is that MBA instructors should not attempt to compete with students on practical industry experience.

Many MBA students possess substantial managerial and organizational experience, often exceeding that of academically trained instructors.

Accordingly, the instructor’s comparative advantage lies in providing:

- Rigorous conceptual frameworks  
- Structured analytical lenses  
- Evidence-based managerial models  
- Academic abstraction and synthesis of complex organizational phenomena  

---

### Framework-Based Instructional Emphasis

Accordingly, the course increasingly emphasizes strategic and conceptual frameworks such as:

- Five Forces  
- Value Chain Analysis  
- Resource-Based View  
- IS Strategy Triangle  
- Game Theory / Strategic Thinking  

These frameworks help students interpret practical managerial experiences through structured analytical lenses.

## Initial Course Development and First Delivery (Fall 2025)

### Development Context

GMBA 5885 was my first MBA-level teaching assignment.

To prepare for the initial offering, I first consulted with:

- The previous instructor of the course  
- The MBA Program Director  

These discussions clarified program expectations, student profile, and the flexibility available in course design.

---

### Course Design Process

Course content was developed based on:

- **Pearlson et al. (8th ed.)**  
  *Managing and Using Information Systems: A Strategic Approach*

- **Laudon et al. (18th ed.)**  
  *Management Information Systems: Managing the Digital Firm*

- Prior and current course syllabi from Kean University and Wenzhou-Kean University

Based on these references, I selectively curated and reorganized topics to better fit the MBA audience and course objectives.

---

### Major Curriculum Innovations Introduced

#### AI Integration into Group Project

A major redesign initiative was integrating AI into the core group project.

Students were required to:

- Analyze an organization's existing MIS  
- Identify business/operational challenges  
- Propose an AI-enabled innovation to address those issues  

This shifted the project from descriptive MIS analysis toward strategic innovation and contemporary managerial relevance.

---

#### Dedicated AI Module ("AI Day")

Introduced a dedicated instructional unit on AI, initially focusing on:

- AI applications in business  
- Prompt engineering / effective AI use  
- General AI opportunities in MIS contexts

### Reflection and Issues Identified

After Fall 2025, several improvement opportunities were identified:

- Project requirements required further clarification and tighter scope control  
- AI instruction was overly focused on prompt engineering/basic usage  
- Some sessions remained too lecture-heavy for MBA audience  
- Greater use of students’ own professional experience was needed  

---

## Major Revision Cycle (Spring 2026)

### Project Redesign

Revised group project requirements based on common issues observed in Fall 2025 submissions/presentations.

Key improvements included:

- Clarified analytical expectations  
- Better defined AI innovation scope  
- Strengthened requirements for business justification and implementation logic  

---

### Expanded AI Curriculum

Substantially redesigned the AI module.

#### Morning Session: AI in Organizations

Shifted away from textbook-based AI coverage toward practical managerial relevance.

Topics included:

- What AI is and is not  
- How AI creates business value  
- AI opportunities across industries/business functions  
- Strategic identification of AI use cases in students’ organizations  

---

#### Afternoon Session: AI at the Individual Level

Expanded beyond prompt engineering.

Introduced the **4D AI Fluency Framework**, emphasizing:

- Identifying AI-augmentable workflows  
- Designing reusable AI skills/processes  
- Building lightweight agents/workflows  
- Moving beyond isolated prompts toward systematic human-AI collaboration  

---

### Pedagogical Adaptations

Greater emphasis was placed on leveraging students’ own industry experience.

For example, in digital transformation sessions:

Rather than relying solely on lecture/case discussion, students were asked to:

- Discuss digital transformation initiatives from their own organizations/industries  
- Present projects they had managed or participated in  
- Connect course frameworks to lived managerial experience  

This substantially improved engagement and practical relevance.

---

## Intellectual Rigor Enhancement (Spring 2026 and Beyond)

To further strengthen the intellectual challenge dimension of the course, I introduced modules on:

- Game Theory  
- Strategic Thinking  

This leverages my own research expertise while maintaining managerial relevance.

The addition reflects my view that MBA courses should not only be practical, but also intellectually demanding.

---

## Ongoing Development Priorities

Planned future refinements include:

- Further localization of case materials with more China/Asia business examples  
- Continued refinement of experiential learning formats  
- Deeper integration of AI strategy with MIS/competitive strategy frameworks  
- Further improvement in balancing conceptual rigor and practitioner relevance
